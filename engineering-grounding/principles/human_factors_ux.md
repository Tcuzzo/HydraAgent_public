# Human Factors & UX — Grounding Principles

Verified guardrails for any surface a human touches. The mission is elite results for the priced-out "common man," so usability is an engineering requirement, not polish — this grounds invariant 11 ("meet the human").

- **Build surfaces to established usability heuristics: visibility of system status, match the user's real-world language, error PREVENTION over error messages, recognition over recall, obvious affordances, and the principle of least astonishment — with immediate feedback so the human never has to learn the system's internals to act.** Why: Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics; Norman, *The Design of Everyday Things* (affordances, feedback, mapping); Principle of Least Astonishment. Guards against surfaces that leak internal jargon/IDs/state enums, hide status, punish errors instead of preventing them, or force the human up to the system's level instead of coming down to theirs.
